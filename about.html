Why RoundSignal exists

If you sell services to startups — development, design, recruiting, growth, PR, fractional leadership — your best customers are the ones who just raised money. Fresh capital, plans bigger than the team, a deadline running. But finding those companies every week means reading a dozen funding sources, deduping them, working out who to contact and why now. It's an hour or two a day of unglamorous work, so most people skip it and lose the timing.

RoundSignal does that reading for you. Every Monday it pulls the fresh raises into one scored list: company, round, investors, who to contact, and an angle to open with. You get the timing without the grind.

Built in public, by one founder

RoundSignal is a small, independent project — one founder, no big team behind it, no venture backing. That's on purpose: it keeps the product honest and the list unpadded. You're not the audience for a data-broker upsell; you're someone who needs a good shortlist on a Monday morning.

Because it's built in public, the way the list is made is public too — you can read exactly how accounts are sourced and scored. Nothing is scraped from private data; everything traces back to a public funding announcement with a source link.

The promise

  • Every Monday, on time. Fresh raises, scored, ready to act on.
  • Honest lists. A quiet week is a short list, not a padded one.
  • No lock-in. The current issue is always free to preview, and paid plans cancel anytime.
